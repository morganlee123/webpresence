Morgan Sandler

Technical Research Staff @ RAND | Computer Science Ph.D. Student @ MSU

Morgan Sandler is a Technical Policy Analyst in RAND Corporation's Engineering and Applied Sciences Department, specializing in science and technology policy and national security applications. His research spans chemical and biological threat mitigation, AI-enabled non-proliferation analysis, nation-state technology competition on connected and autonomous vehicles, responsible AI use in high-conflict scenarios, biosecurity risk assessment, and benchmarking LLM legal reasoning abilities.

Currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Computer Science at Michigan State University, Sandler develops methodological approaches for emotion-aware LLMs with enhanced theory of mind capabilities. His doctoral research includes comparative analyses of human versus LLM-generated empathic conversations and pioneering work in affective computing applications.

Sandler bridges rigorous policy analysis with cutting-edge AI research to address both technical possibilities and societal implications of emerging technologies in national security contexts.

Research Areas

Emotion-Aware AI & Theory of Mind

Developing methodological approaches for creating LLMs with enhanced theory of mind capabilities that can accurately perceive and respond to human emotional cues. Research includes comparative analyses of human versus LLM-generated empathic conversations and advancing affective computing applications.

AI Policy & National Security

Conducting policy analysis on AI applications in defense and homeland security contexts, including responsible AI use in high-conflict scenarios, chemical and biological threat mitigation, and AI-enabled non-proliferation analysis.

Technology Competition Analysis

Analyzing nation-state science and technology competition, particularly focusing on connected and autonomous vehicles, and developing frameworks to assess global technological capabilities and competitive dynamics.

AI Risk Assessment

Evaluating biosecurity risks posed by AI systems and benchmarking large language model capabilities in legal reasoning and compliance contexts.
