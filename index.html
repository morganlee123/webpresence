About Me

I am a computer scientist and a technical analyst at RAND, specializing in AI policy, ethical alignment, and defense research. My work is interdisciplinary, bridging cognitive science, computer science, philosophy, and public policy to ensure that AI systems align with human values, particularly in defense and homeland security contexts. I hold an M.Sc. in Computer Science from Michigan State University, where I focused on speaker recognition (biometrics), and am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Computer Science (Artificial Intelligence) supervised by Professor Arun Ross. View my full profile and research on my RAND page or visit my Google Scholar profile.

Research

Compassionate Computing: Exploring how large language models can exhibit empathy and prosocial behaviors to improve human-AI collaboration in diverse settings, from education to defense.

Ethical Measurement in LLMs: Developing quantitative and qualitative frameworks to assess and enhance ethical behaviors in language models.

Trust in AI for Defense: Investigating metrics to evaluate warfighter trust in AI systems, including novel trust metrics and risk aversion analysis, targeted at DOD stakeholders.

Nation-State Competitive Analysis: Constructing frameworks to assess global science and technology capabilities, focusing on drivers of change, metrics, and data sources for competitive analysis.

LLMs and Legal Compliance: Examining the extent to which large language models can recognize and adhere to U.S. laws, including challenges in interpreting legal requirements, identifying restricted actions, and understanding compliance contexts.
